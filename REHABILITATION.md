A rehabilitation schedule for distal biceps repair is divided into phases to protect the surgical site while gradually restoring movement and strength.

Phase 1: Protection & Early Motion (Weeks 0–6) 
The primary goal is protecting the repair while maintaining hand, wrist, and shoulder function. 
PubMed Central (PMC) (.gov)
PubMed Central (PMC) (.gov)
Weeks 0–2:
Immobilization: Arm is typically kept in a splint or brace locked at 90°.
Exercises: Active range of motion (ROM) for fingers and wrist. Shoulder shrugs and gentle shoulder ROM are often encouraged to prevent stiffness.
Weeks 2–6:
Brace Adjustment: The brace may be gradually opened to allow more motion.
Exercises: Passive elbow flexion (using the other hand to bend the elbow) and active-assisted elbow extension (within brace limits).
Grip Work: Gentle gripping exercises using putty or a soft ball.

Phase 2: Intermediate Strengthening (Weeks 6–12) 
Once the tendon has sufficiently scarred into the bone, active movement begins. 
Surgical Recovery Systems
Surgical Recovery Systems
Weeks 6–8:
Weaning off Brace: The brace is typically discontinued.
Exercises: Active elbow flexion and forearm rotation (supination/pronation) without weight. Light isometric triceps exercises (pushing down against a table).
Weeks 8–12:
Introduction of Weight: Begin light resisted exercises using 1–2 lb weights or very light resistance bands for elbow flexion and supination.
Total Body: Resuming cardiovascular fitness (stationary bike, treadmill) without using the operative arm.

Phase 3: Advanced Strengthening & Return to Sport (Weeks 12–26)
Focus shifts to regaining full strength and preparing for high-impact activities. 
Surgical Recovery Systems
Surgical Recovery Systems
Weeks 12–16:
Progressive Loading: Increase weight for biceps curls and forearm rotation.
Functional Tasks: Begin incorporating everyday lifting tasks and eccentric loading (lowering weight slowly).
Weeks 16–26:
Unrestricted Activity: Gradual return to heavy lifting and contact sports.
Sport-Specific Drills: Athletes begin tailored drills to simulate game movements under tension.
